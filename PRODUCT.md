# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Two user types, weighted equally:

- **Homeowners** looking for a specific home job done (plumbing, electrical, carpentry, painting, masonry, AC, etc.). Job to be done: quickly find a verified, well-rated majstor (tradesperson) nearby and send a request with minimal friction.
- **Majstori (tradespeople)** looking for work. Job to be done: get discovered, receive qualified leads/requests, and manage their profile and reputation (ratings, status badge) to win more jobs.

DomFix only succeeds if both sides are well served — sourcing demand (homeowners) and supply (majstori) matter equally.

## Product Purpose

DomFix is a Serbian-language marketplace that connects homeowners with verified local tradespeople. A homeowner posts what they need; majstori in that category/area can be found, contacted, and booked directly. Success looks like: homeowners trust the listings enough to request work without hesitation, and majstori treat DomFix as a real lead pipeline worth maintaining their profile for (ratings, status badge, responsiveness).

## Brand Personality

Warm, local, community-feeling — like asking a trusted neighbor for a recommendation, not a corporate services portal. Reference points: TaskRabbit and Thumbtack for the task-matching mechanics and efficiency, but DomFix should read warmer and more local/neighborhood than either — less "gig platform," more "your local community knows good people."

## Anti-references

None specified yet. Avoid generic, corporate-feeling marketplace tropes (cold blue-on-white SaaS look, stock-photo gloss) that would undercut the warm/local positioning.

## Design Principles

- **Trust before transaction**: verification, ratings, and status badges should be visible and legible before a homeowner is asked to commit to a request.
- **Equal footing for both sides**: the majstor-facing flows (leads, profile, requests) deserve the same design care as the homeowner-facing discovery flows — this is not a consumer app with a thin pro-side bolted on.
- **Local and human, not corporate**: warmth comes from tone, language, and imagery choices, not gloss or polish-for-its-own-sake.
- **Friction-free requests**: posting a job and reaching a majstor should never feel like more effort than calling a neighbor for a recommendation.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA and beyond where it costs little: homeowners requesting trade work may skew older or less tech-savvy, so favor larger touch targets, plain and unambiguous Serbian (Latin script) copy, and forgiving form validation over terse/clever UI. Standard reduced-motion support applies.
